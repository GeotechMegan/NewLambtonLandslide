"The basic theory of landslide processes is well understood. However, landslide risk assessments are complex...local governments have principal responsibility for managing landslide risk. ... Often a number of factors will contribute to a landslide occurring...frequently, the dominant factor...will be an increase in pore water pressure from rainfall or leaking infrastructure. Other triggers include...undercutting of slopes by fluvial erosion" -Chapter Eight: Landslide, NATURAL HAZARDS in AUSTRALIA, by Geoscience Australia's Risk and Impact Analysis Group, 2007, commissioned by COAG (Council of Australian Government).
